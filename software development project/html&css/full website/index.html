Allergies

Allergies occur when the immune system reacts to a foreign substance, known as an allergen, that is typically harmless. Common allergens include pollen, pet dander, certain foods, and medications.

Cancer

It can start almost anywhere, including the skin, organs, bones, and blood. There are over 100 types of cancer, and common signs and symptoms may include lumps, abnormal bleeding, prolonged cough, unexplained weight loss, and changes in bowel movements

Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is a chronic autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin, requiring lifelong management through insulin therapy and lifestyle adjustments.
